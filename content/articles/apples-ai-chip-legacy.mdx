<h2>Introduction to Apple's Neural Engine</h2><p>Apple's self-driving car program may have stalled, but it left a lasting legacy in the form of powerful AI chips. The company's early efforts to develop a self-driving platform led to the creation of the Neural Engine, a dedicated AI processing unit that has become the backbone of Apple's on-device AI capabilities.</p><h3>What is the Neural Engine?</h3><p>The Neural Engine is a specialized chip designed to handle the complex computations required for machine learning and artificial intelligence. It was first introduced in the A11 Bionic chip and has since been improved upon in subsequent iterations, including the M7 Ultra.</p><h2>How the Neural Engine Works</h2><p>The Neural Engine is designed to work in conjunction with Apple's other processing units, such as the CPU and GPU, to provide a comprehensive AI processing solution. It uses a combination of hardware and software optimizations to accelerate machine learning tasks, making it possible to run complex AI models on-device.</p><h3>Benefits for Developers</h3><p>The Neural Engine has significant implications for developers, particularly those working on AI-powered apps and services.
